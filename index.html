01 Homepage

Make the homepage product-led

The homepage opens with brand mission. That’s fine — but a visitor who wants to buy has no obvious next step above the fold.

⚡ Pre-launch priority

The two problems

🔴
No CTA above the fold
The hero video works well as brand-building, but there is no button, no link, and no prompt to shop until the user scrolls past the full-screen video. For any visitor arriving from an ad or social post, this is the first drop-off point.
⚠️
Category shortcuts exist — but are buried mid-page
Sports Performance, Health & Lifestyle, Pain Relief, and Recovery are already on the homepage — they just appear too far down. These are the 4 pillars customers should see immediately after the hero.

What to change

  • Add a primary CTA button in the hero section — e.g. “Shop All Products” or “Find Your Chip”. Sits below the existing tagline, no redesign needed.
  • Add a plain-English one-liner beneath the headline: e.g. “Wearable bio-signalling chips for performance, recovery and wellbeing.”
  • Move the 4 category blocks (Sports, Lifestyle, Pain Relief, Recovery) higher — the first thing visible after the hero, each linking directly to its category page.
  • Add a trust strip beneath the category blocks: “Drug-Free · Non-Invasive · 14-Day Money-Back · UK Company” — one line, no design effort needed.

02 Navigation

Add a Products dropdown

Right now “Products” goes straight to a flat list of 42 items. A dropdown with the 4 pillars lets customers navigate directly to what’s relevant to them.

⚡ Pre-launch priority
🔴
Missing navigation shortcut
A customer interested in pain relief has to land on the full 42-product listing and filter manually. A simple dropdown removes this friction entirely. Wix supports dropdown menus natively in the menu editor — no code required.

Recommended dropdown structure

Each item links to an existing category URL (/category/sports-enhancement, /category/lifestyle-improvement, /category/pain-relief-recovery). “View All” at the bottom keeps the full listing accessible. Set up in Wix Menu Manager — no code needed.

How to set this up in Wix

  • Go to Site & App → Site Pages & Menu → Main Menu
  • Click the “Products” menu item → select “Add Sub-item”
  • Add the category pages as sub-items, plus “All Products” at the bottom
  • The dropdown appears automatically on hover — no CSS or code required
✅
Quick win — under 15 minutes to implement
The category pages already exist and have URLs. Adding the dropdown is purely a Wix menu editor task — no design work, no code, no images needed.

One additional navigation note

⚠️
“The Movement” in the main nav
This page is a deep-dive science explainer with no call to action — valuable content, but it’s not a sales page. Consider moving it under “Company” as a sub-item, or at minimum adding a “Shop the technology →” CTA button at the top of the page. A 5-minute change.
03 Product Pages

Build trust at the point of purchase

The product pages are where the decision is made. Right now they’re missing the two things every buyer needs: a reason to trust, and answers to their obvious questions.

⚡ Pre-launch priority

Current issues

🔴
No trust signals near the buy button
The Add to Cart button sits alone with no supporting context. The 14-day money-back guarantee exists — but only in a small text block at the top of the listing page. A buyer on the product page never sees it. This needs to be right at the moment of decision.
🔴
No FAQ section — unanswered questions = abandoned carts
For a novel product like NRG+, customers will have questions: How do I wear it? Is it legal in sport? How long does it last? What if it doesn’t work? If those questions aren’t on the page, the customer either emails (friction) or leaves (lost sale).
⚠️
Copy leads with biology, not benefits
The descriptions explain aerobic metabolism and Type I muscle fibres — useful science, but not what a buyer needs first. Lead with the outcome in plain English, then support with science below. A small reorder, not a full rewrite.

FAQ content for all product pages

How do I wear the chip?
Place it directly on the skin or inside your clothing or kit, close to the body. Adhesive strips are included with every chip.
Is it legal for competitive sport?
Yes. NRG+ chips contain no substances — they are passive, non-invasive wearable technology. Nothing to declare, nothing to test for.
How quickly will I notice a difference?
Most users notice changes within the first 1–3 sessions. Results vary by individual — which is why we back every purchase with a 14-day money-back guarantee.
Can I wear it in water?
The chip is water-resistant. Avoid prolonged submersion.
What’s the difference between 2nd Gen and 3rd Gen?
The 2nd Gen chip lasts 12 months (£119). The 3rd Gen is designed to last a lifetime (£147). At £147, the 3rd Gen works out at roughly 10p/day after year one.
What if it doesn’t work for me?
We offer a 14-day money-back guarantee. Email Sales@EnhancedNRG.com and we’ll sort it — no questions asked.

05 Post-launch

Parked for after launch

Real improvements — but they need more time than the launch window allows. Revisit these in the weeks after go-live.

📅 Post-launch

Social proof & reviews

Month 1
  • Install a review widget (Wix Reviews or Trustpilot) and email all existing customers for reviews — offer 10% off their next order as incentive
  • Replace anonymous homepage testimonials with photos, full names, sport, and social handles
  • Embed Instagram or TikTok athlete content directly on product pages

Product listing improvements

Month 1
  • Add a generation comparison table to the listing page (Tester vs 2nd Gen vs 3rd Gen: price, lifespan, best for)
  • Replace the “New” badge on every product with meaningful labels: ‘Most Popular’, ‘Best Seller’, etc.
  • Move out-of-stock products to the bottom of the listing with an “email me when back” option
  • Add a short guided quiz to the listing page to reduce decision paralysis

Email & retention

Months 1–2
  • Set up abandoned cart email automation in Wix Automations — recovers 5–15% of abandoned carts
  • Set up a post-purchase onboarding email: “Welcome to NRG+ — here’s how to get the most from your chip”
  • Set up a 14-day review request email triggered after each purchase
  • Replace the newsletter CTA with a value-exchange offer: e.g. “Get our free performance guide + 10% off”

Longer-term

Months 2–3
  • Build sport-specific landing pages (Runners, Gym Athletes, Golfers, Cyclists) for paid ad campaigns
  • Set up a Trustpilot profile — verified reviews display star ratings in Google search results
  • Add cross-sell section on product pages: “Customers also bought the Recovery Chip”
  • Add an order bump at checkout: e.g. “Add the Recovery Chip for £95 (save £24)”
